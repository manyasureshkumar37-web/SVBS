Mysuru Inside Places

Sand Sculpture Museum

It is a unique tourist attraction in Mysuru where over 150 sculptures made completely from sand are displayed. It is considered India’s first museum dedicated entirely to sand art. It was founded by M.N.Gowri, a Mysuru-based sand artist.

Sand Sculpture Museum

The Mysuru Sand Sculpture Museum features unique sand sculptures.

Payana Car Museum

The word “Payana” means journey in Kannada. The museum represents a journey through the evolution of automobiles—from early vintage vehicles to classic mid-20th-century cars. It aims to preserve and showcase the engineering, design, and cultural significance of historic vehicles.

Payana Car Museum

Vintage, Automobiles, History, Heritage, Exhibition.

Lalitha Mahal Palace

Lalitha Mahal Palace is one of the most beautiful royal buildings in Mysuru. It is the second largest palace in Mysuru after the Mysore Palace and is famous for its grand white architecture and luxury heritage hotel.It was originally built as a guest palace for the Viceroy of India.

Lalitha Mahal Palace

Lalitha Mahal Palace is a beautiful royal palace.

Melody World Wax Museum

The Melody World Wax Museum in Mysuru is a unique museum dedicated to music and musicians. It displays life-size wax statues of musicians along with thousands of musical instruments from around the world.

Melody World Wax Museum

Melody World Wax Museum displays musical wax statues and instruments.

St.Philomena's Church

St. Philomena's Cathedral is one of the largest churches in India. It was built in 1936 in Neo-Gothic architectural style. The church has tall twin spires and beautiful stained-glass windows. These windows depict important events from the life of Jesus Christ.

St. Philomena's Cathedral

St. Philomena's Cathedral is a Gothic-style church.

Railway Museum

The Railway Museum in Mysuru showcases the history of Indian Railways. It displays vintage locomotives, royal coaches, and railway equipment. Visitors can see the luxurious coach once used by the Mysore Maharajas. A toy train ride is also available for children and families.

Railway Museum

Mysuru Railway Museum showcases vintage trains and railway heritage.

Karanji Lake

Karanji Lake is a peaceful nature spot located near Mysore Zoo. It is surrounded by greenery and is perfect for birdwatching and boating. The lake also has a large walk-through aviary and a butterfly park. It is a great place for relaxing and enjoying nature.

Karanji Lake

Karanji Lake is a serene lake with a butterfly park and bird sanctuary.

Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary is located about 18 km from Mysuru. It is the largest bird sanctuary in Karnataka and home to many migratory birds. Birds such as pelicans, painted storks, and kingfishers can be seen here. Visitors can take boat rides to watch birds and crocodiles closely.

Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary is a vibrant riverside haven for migratory birds.

GRS Fantasy Park

GRS Fantasy Park is a popular amusement and water park in Mysuru. It offers exciting rides, water slides, and wave pools. The park has separate areas for kids and family entertainment. It is a perfect place for fun outings with friends and family.

GRS Fantasy Park

GRS Fantasy Park is a fun-filled adventure park with rides and water attractions.

Krishna Raja Sagara Dam

Krishna Raja Sagara Dam, commonly known as KRS Dam, is built across the River Kaveri. It was constructed under the guidance of Sir M. Visvesvaraya. The dam creates a large reservoir that supplies water for irrigation and drinking. It is also famous for the nearby Brindavan Gardens.

Krishna Raja Sagara Dam

Krishna Raja Sagara Dam is a majestic dam surrounded by gardens and scenic views.

Brindavan Garden

Brindavan Gardens is located below the KRS Dam near Mysuru. It is known for its beautifully designed terraces, fountains, and gardens. The musical fountain show in the evening is the main attraction. The colorful lights and water show attract thousands of visitors.

Brindavan Garden

Brindavan Gardens is famous for musical fountains and illuminated landscapes.

Jaganmohan Palace

Jaganmohan Palace is a historic palace built in 1861 by the Mysore royal family. Today it serves as the Sri Jayachamarajendra Art Gallery. The gallery displays paintings, sculptures, and rare artifacts. It also houses famous artworks by Raja Ravi Varma.

Jaganmohan Palace

Jaganmohan Palace houses royal art and exquisite traditional paintings.

Melkote

Melkote is a historic temple town located about 50 km from Mysuru. It is famous for the Cheluvanarayana Swamy Temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The town also has the Yoga Narasimha Temple on a hilltop. Melkote is known for its spiritual atmosphere and beautiful landscapes.

Melkote

Melkote is a historic town known for its hilltop temples.
